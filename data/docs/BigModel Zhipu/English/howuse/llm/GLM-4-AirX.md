## GLM-4-AirX

GLM-4-AirX is the high-performance version of GLM-4-Air. It inherits the multilingual understanding and complex reasoning capabilities of the GLM-4 base model while featuring ultra-fast inference speed and powerful reasoning performance.

| **Price** | **Input Modality** | **Output Modality** | **Context Window** | **Maximum Output Tokens** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 10 RMB per million tokens | Text | Text | 8K | 4K |

### **Recommended Use Cases**

1. **Intelligent Customer Service System:** Capable of handling massive volumes of user inquiries simultaneously, with millisecond-level response times to customer questions about product features, order status, after-sales policies, etc. It accurately understands multilingual inquiries and quickly accesses the enterpriseâ€™s business knowledge base to generate professional answers, effectively improving customer service efficiency and satisfaction.
2. **Real-Time Data Analysis:** When dealing with high-frequency real-time data streams such as financial trading data or industrial sensor data, it can perform low-latency inference and analysis under high concurrency, quickly identifying data anomalies, predicting trend changes, and generating actionable decision recommendations by integrating industry knowledge. (Recommended to combine with [search tools](https://www.bigmodel.cn/dev/howuse/websearch) for more real-time, accurate, and updated answers.)
3. **Mathematical Problem Reasoning:** In online math education platforms or research assistant scenarios, it can handle highly concurrent math problem-solving requests, performing millisecond-level analysis of complex math problems such as algebraic proofs, geometric modeling, and calculus derivations.

### **Detailed Description**

GLM-4-AirX is specifically designed for low-latency, high-concurrency scenarios. It was pre-trained on 15T of high-quality data, including a rich set of synthetic reasoning data that laid the foundation for subsequent reinforcement learning extensions. In the post-training phase, in addition to human preference alignment for dialogue scenarios, we used techniques such as rejection sampling and reinforcement learning to enhance the modelâ€™s performance in tasks like instruction following, engineering code generation, and function calling, thereby strengthening the fundamental capabilities required for agent tasks.

While maintaining the same performance as GLM-4-Air-250414, the model underwent technical iterations in its core components. Its inference process includes two stagesâ€”prefill and autoregressive decoder outputâ€”which give GLM-4-AirX faster inference speed and more powerful reasoning capabilities.
